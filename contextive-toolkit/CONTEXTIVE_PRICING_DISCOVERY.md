# Pricing discovery — call script (v0.1)

**Status:** internal, local-only (not committed — like CONTEXTIVE_API_PLAN.md). Drafted 2026-06-22.
**Goal:** pressure-test the live launch tiers (Free / **$39** Starter / **$149** Pro / custom) against
real beachhead buyers before we treat them as settled. Find the true willingness-to-pay, the one or two
must-have features, and the language buyers use for the problem. *Measure, don't estimate — including price.*

> We are **testing, not selling.** No discounts, no "what would make you buy today." Capture verbatims.
> 5–10 calls is enough to see the pattern. Beachhead only: comms / PR / public-affairs, and brand / trend
> intelligence (agency or in-house). Skip finance for now.

## Who to call (target 6–10)
- Comms / public-affairs strategists (in-house or agency) who track messaging.
- Brand / cultural-trend analysts (CPG, agency trend desks).
- 1–2 newsroom / research contacts for the credibility read (not the revenue read).

## The call (20–25 min)

**1. Open (1 min).** "I'm researching how teams track whether a message or term is gaining ground. Not
selling anything — I want to understand your world and react to an idea. Can I ask how you work today?"

**2. Their world + pain (5 min).** Listen, don't pitch.
- How do you know if a framing/term is winning or fading right now?
- Walk me through the last time you needed that. What did you do?
- What's annoying or slow about it? What do you *wish* you could see?
→ *Listen for:* "we guess / we wait for polling / we eyeball social / we pay an agency."

**3. Current tools + spend (3 min).** Anchor on real budget.
- What do you use today (Meltwater, Brandwatch, Google Trends, an agency, nothing)?
- Roughly what does your team spend per month on this kind of monitoring?
→ *This is the real WTP anchor — what they already pay beats any stated number.*

**4. React to the concept (3 min).** One sentence + the artifact.
- "Contextive measures how fast a phrase is gaining or losing ground in the open news/media record —
  velocity, not verdict — and lets you watch terms and compare two head-to-head, as an API or a view."
- Show the `obliterated` vs `ceasefire` example. "Useful? Where would you use it? What's missing?"
→ *Listen for the unprompted use case and the first feature they ask for.*

**5. Price reaction — Van Westendorp (4 questions, 4 min).** Ask all four, in order, capture the numbers:
- At what monthly price would this be **so expensive** you wouldn't consider it?
- At what price would it be **expensive but worth considering**?
- At what price would it feel like a **bargain — clearly good value**?
- At what price would it be **so cheap you'd doubt the quality**?
→ The "expensive-but-worth-it" and "bargain" answers bracket the acceptable range; the cross-over of the
curves (across all calls) is the optimal point. Compare against our $39 / $149.

**6. Tier fit (3 min).** "If Starter is ~10 phrases watched and Pro ~50 — which fits, and why? What would
push you from one to the other?" → *Validates the tracked-term unit and the 10-vs-50 split.*

**7. Close (1 min).** "What would have to be true for your team to pay for this?" Then: waitlist, and ask
for one referral to someone with the same problem.

## Capture per call (one row each)
`name/role · current tool + monthly spend · the use case in their words · 4 VW prices · Starter/Pro fit ·
must-have feature · the one objection · referral?`

## Decide after the batch
- If "expensive-but-worth-it" clusters **above $149** → we're underpriced; raise Pro.
- If buyers anchor on **per-seat** or **per-report**, not per-tracked-term → revisit the billing unit.
- If the first must-have is **alerts/webhooks** → pull Phase 3 forward; if **compare()** → it's the hero.
- If they already pay **$500–$2k/mo** for monitoring → an enterprise tier is the real revenue, not Starter.
